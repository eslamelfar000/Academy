50 Years of the Z-Score Family of Distress Prediction Models

About the program

Fifty-five years ago, Dr. Edward I. Altman published the initial, classic version of the Z-score bankruptcy prediction models.

This multivariate statistical model has remained perhaps the most well-known, and more importantly, most used technique for providing an early warning signal of firm financial distress by academics and practtioners on a global basis.

Program Objectives

Understanding the Z-Score Altman model, enabling him/ her to evaluate opportunities to benefit from it and applying it in his/ her field of work.

Identify the different activities that can use the Z-Score Altman model to assess early warning signs of financial distress.
